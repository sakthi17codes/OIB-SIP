Queen Velu Naachiyaar - இராணி வேலு நாச்சியார்


First Queen to Battle against British

1730:Birth

Velu Nachiyar was born to the royal couple of Ramanathapuram (Sivagangai), Raja Chellamuthu Vijayaragunatha Sethupathy and Rani Sakandhimuthathal.

1750:Marriage

Velu Nachiyar married Muthuvaduganatha Periya Udaiyathevar, the prince of Sivagangai

1772:Death of her husband

Muthuvaduganatha Periya Udaiyathevar was killed by British forces led by Colonel Joseph Smith, supported by the Nawab of Arcot.

1772-1780:Strategic Alliances and Training

Velu Nachiyar spent this period forming alliances with neighboring kingdoms and training an army to retaliate against the British. During this time, she learned and adopted various military tactics.

1780:Rebellion Against the British:

Velu Nachiyar successfully led a rebellion against the British. With the help of her allies, including Gopala Nayaker and Hyder Ali of Mysore, she launched an attack on the British forces and recaptured Sivagangai.

1780-1790:Rule as Queen

Velu Nachiyar ruled Sivagangai from 1780 until her death. She implemented effective administrative measures and continued to resist British influence in her territory.

1780:Formation of the First Human Bomb

Velu Nachiyar is credited with devising the first recorded instance of a suicide attack in Indian history. Her loyal follower, Kuyili, doused herself in ghee and set herself ablaze to destroy the British ammunition depot, playing a crucial role in Velu Nachiyar's successful campaign.

1790:Death

Velu Nachiyar passed away, leaving behind a legacy of courage and resistance against colonialism.

Posthumous Recognition

Statues and Memorials

Various statues and memorials have been erected in her honor, particularly in Tamil Nadu

Commemoration by the Indian Government

In 2008, the Government of India released a commemorative postage stamp in her honor.

Symbol of Empowerment

Using Rani Velu Nachiyar as a symbol in initiatives like Khelo India highlights the values of bravery, leadership, and empowerment. It serves multiple purposes, from promoting gender equality in sports to instilling national pride and preserving cultural heritage.
